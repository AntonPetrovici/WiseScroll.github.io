Behind WiseScroll

About Me

Hi, I’m Anton, a student at UWC Robert Bosch College from Moldova and the creator of WiseScroll.

I built this project because I personally faced a problem that I think many people can relate to: there are moments when you just want to scroll — to relax, to explore something — but instead you end up doomscrolling content that doesn’t give you anything meaningful.

I wanted an alternative — a way to scroll that still feels effortless, but actually helps you learn, discover, and grow. That’s how WiseScroll started.

My goal is to make discovering research papers feel as natural and engaging as scrolling through your favorite apps, while still keeping the depth and value of real science.

Your main photo

My Story

As a student interested in science and technology, I often found research papers fascinating — but also hard to approach regularly. They require focus, time, and intention, which makes them difficult to integrate into everyday casual browsing.

At the same time, social media made it incredibly easy to consume content — just not the kind that adds real value.

This contrast made me ask a simple question: What if discovering science felt as natural as scrolling TikTok? WiseScroll is my attempt to answer that.

Why I started WiseScroll

There’s a gap between high-quality knowledge and how people actually consume content today. Most platforms are optimized for attention, not understanding, while most scientific platforms are optimized for depth, not accessibility.

WiseScroll tries to combine both: the simplicity and engagement of modern apps with the depth and credibility of real research.

So instead of wasting time scrolling, you can invest it without even noticing.

What inspires me

I’m inspired by the idea that technology can reshape how we learn.

We already have access to incredible knowledge — but access alone isn’t enough. What matters is how naturally that knowledge fits into our daily lives.

I want to build tools that make learning feel effortless, turn curiosity into a habit, and help people explore ideas that actually matter.

What I want to build

WiseScroll is just the beginning.

My vision is to create a platform where discovering research is as easy as scrolling, complex ideas feel intuitive, and time spent online becomes genuinely valuable.

In the future, this can grow into a full ecosystem for learning, discovery, and intellectual curiosity.

Not everything you scroll has to be wasted time.
